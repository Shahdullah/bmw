The Pinnacle of Performance

Engineering Brilliance

627 HP S63 M TwinPower Turbo V8

The S63 engine reaches its absolute zenith in the M5 CS, delivering a staggering 627 horsepower that redefines what is possible in a luxury sedan. This 4.4-liter powerplant is equipped with a cross-bank exhaust manifold that optimizes gas exchange to ensure lightning-fast turbo response across the entire rev range. Every component has been refined to provide relentless thrust, making it the most powerful production engine in the history of BMW M. It isn't just about the raw numbers; it's about the precision of power delivery that remains consistent even under extreme track conditions. The cooling system has been further optimized with an additional oil cooler to maintain performance during prolonged high-speed sessions.

2.9s Supercar Acceleration

The M5 CS shatters boundaries with a 0-60 mph sprint of just 2.9 seconds, a figure that places it firmly in the territory of dedicated mid-engine supercars. This explosive performance is managed by the intelligent M xDrive all-wheel-drive system, which can be adjusted from a balanced AWD setting to a pure 2WD drift mode for the ultimate purist experience. The 8-speed M Steptronic transmission provides instantaneous gear shifts, ensuring that power flow to the wheels is never interrupted. This combination of traction and transmission speed allows the CS to dominate both the drag strip and the most technical sections of the Nürburgring. Detailed launch control software manages engine torque and tire slip for the perfect takeoff every time.

-154 lbs CFRP Lightweight Architecture

Weight is the enemy of performance, and the M5 CS has undergone a rigorous diet to shed 154 lbs compared to the M5 Competition. Extensive use of Carbon Fiber Reinforced Plastic (CFRP) is visible throughout the car, from the vented hood to the front splitter, mirror caps, and the aggressive rear diffuser. Even the interior features lightweight carbon bucket seats that provide exceptional lateral support while contributing to the overall mass reduction. This lightweight philosophy drastically improves the car's power-to-weight ratio, resulting in sharper turn-in, reduced braking distances, and a level of agility that belies its sedan dimensions. The reduced unsprung mass at the wheels further improves suspension response on uneven surfaces.
